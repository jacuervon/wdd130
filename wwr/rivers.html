Desolation and Gray Canyon, Utah

The Green River in central Utah, known for its high cliffs and desert scenery, flows through the desolate Desolation Canyon towards the Colorado River. The 84-mile journey from Sand Wash to Swaseys Rapid offers stunning landscapes, geologic formations, and traces of human history.

The canyon, a Class II/III whitewater destination typically explored in 6 days, is a National Landmark recognized for its unique and historical significance.

Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

The Salmon River, located in the Frank Church Wilderness of No Return, is often referred to as the River of No Return due to its captivating beauty. The river is home to diverse wildlife, including elk, deer, bear, and bighorn sheep, and is lined with towering ponderosa pines.

The river’s rapids, ranging from class I-IV, offer varying experiences throughout the summer. The Salmon River, one of the longest undammed rivers in the U.S., is particularly appreciated by families for its slower, warmer waters in late summer.

Grand Canyon, Arizona

The Grand Canyon, the ultimate of all canyons and river trips, offers an unforgettable experience with its world-class whitewater rafting and stunning scenery.

The canyon is not only filled with thrilling rapids, but also side canyons and ancient Indian ruins that are only accessible by river. The Grand Canyon uses a unique I-X river rating scale to accommodate its wide variety of rapids, although the regular I-V scale is often referred to for familiarity.
